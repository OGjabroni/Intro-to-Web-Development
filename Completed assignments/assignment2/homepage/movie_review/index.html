Magruber

  • Directed by: Jorma Taccone
  • Released 2010

Starring

  • Will Forte
  • Kristen Wiig
  • Ryan Phillippe
  • Val Kilmer

First of all, let's talk about the titular character, MacGruber, played by the incomparable Will Forte. This guy is a true visionary. He's like the love child of James Bond, MacGyver, and a pack of dynamite, and his mission is to save the world. But he's also a bit of a doofus, and that's where the magic happens.

The plot? Well, it's there, I guess. Something about nuclear warheads and a villain named Dieter Von Cunth, played by Val Kilmer in a performance that is nothing short of jaw-droppingly bizarre. It's like he walked onto the set and said, "I'm going to be the weirdest thing in this movie," and he succeeded with flying colors.

Kristen Wigg is in the movie

But what truly sets MacGruber apart is its fearless commitment to absurdity. It's like they said, "You know what this movie needs? A montage of MacGruber and his team dancing to 'Take Me Home Tonight' by Eddie Money in a cemetery. And they did it! And it's glorious!

And let's not forget the celery, folks. MacGruber has an inexplicable obsession with celery sticks, and it becomes a running gag that will have you questioning the very nature of comedy itself. I mean, celery has never been this funny, and I doubt it ever will be again.

MacGruber is a cinematic masterpiece that defies all expectations and delivers pure, unadulterated hilarity. It's a surrealist's dream come true, a triumph of absurdity, and a testament to the power of celery. If you haven't seen it yet, do yourself a favor and experience the brilliance that is MacGruber
